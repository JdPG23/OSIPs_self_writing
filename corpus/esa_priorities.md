# ESA Strategic Priorities (2025-2030)

This document summarizes ESA's current strategic priorities for OSIP alignment.
Update this file periodically with the latest from ESA's official publications.

## Core Programmatic Lines

### 1. Space Safety Programme
- **Space Surveillance and Tracking (SST)**: Monitoring debris and objects in orbit
- **Planetary Defence**: Near-Earth Object detection and deflection (Hera mission follow-up)
- **Space Weather**: Monitoring and early warning systems (Vigil mission)
- **Clean Space**: Active debris removal technologies (ClearSpace-1 follow-up)
- **Collision avoidance automation**: AI-driven manoeuvre planning

### 2. Terrae Novae — Exploration
- **Moon**: European Large Logistic Lander (EL3), Lunar Gateway contributions
- **Mars**: Mars Sample Return, ExoMars rover operations
- **LEO**: ISS decommissioning, commercial LEO destinations
- **Human spaceflight**: Sustainable presence in LEO and cis-lunar
- **ISRU**: In-Situ Resource Utilisation technologies

### 3. Earth Observation
- **Copernicus expansion**: Sentinel fleet evolution, CO2 monitoring
- **Climate monitoring**: Carbon cycle, ice sheets, sea level
- **AI/ML for EO**: On-board processing, data fusion, automated analysis
- **Rapid response**: Disaster monitoring and early warning

### 4. Telecommunications & Navigation
- **Galileo Second Generation**: Enhanced accuracy and authentication
- **EGNOS V3**: Aviation-grade navigation augmentation
- **Optical communications**: Space-to-ground high-bandwidth links
- **Quantum Key Distribution**: Secure space-based communications
- **6G/NTN integration**: Non-terrestrial network standards

### 5. Space Transportation
- **Ariane 6**: Operational reliability and cost reduction
- **Reusability**: THEMIS demonstrator, Prometheus engine
- **In-orbit servicing**: Life extension, refuelling, assembly
- **Micro/nano-launcher ecosystem**: European sovereign access

### 6. Technology & Innovation
- **AI and autonomy**: On-board decision making, mission planning
- **Quantum technologies**: Sensing, computing, communications
- **Advanced manufacturing**: In-space manufacturing, 3D printing
- **Green propulsion**: Electric propulsion, green propellants
- **Digital twins**: Mission simulation and operations

## Current OSIP Focus Areas (from recent calls)

Based on recent OSIP calls and implemented ideas (2023-2025):
- VLEO (Very Low Earth Orbit) technologies
- On-orbit servicing and manufacturing
- Space sustainability and circular economy
- AI for autonomous space operations
- Quantum technologies for space applications
- Bio-inspired solutions for space
- Advanced materials and structures
- Novel propulsion concepts
- Space-based solar power elements
- Debris mitigation and removal

## OSIP Proposal Tips (from successful proposals)

1. **Be specific**: Reference exact ESA missions/programs by name
2. **State TRL clearly**: Entry TRL and target TRL with justification
3. **Budget realistically**: 20-100K EUR for studies, 50-175K EUR for tech dev
4. **Show the gap**: What capability is missing and why it matters to ESA
5. **Differentiate**: What makes this different from existing funded work
6. **Timeline**: 12-18 months is typical for OSIP activities
7. **Acronyms work**: Successful OSIPs often have memorable acronyms (PEACE, MISTRAL, ECHO2)
8. **Spain/ESA connection**: Leverage ESA ESTEC, ESAC, ESRIN proximity if applicable
