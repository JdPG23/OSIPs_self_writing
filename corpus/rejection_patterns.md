# Common OSIP Rejection Patterns

These are common reasons why OSIP proposals get rejected. The Critic Agent
should check for these patterns. Add more as you learn from real feedback.

- Too vague: proposal describes a general area without a specific technical approach
- No clear innovation: describes incremental improvement over existing solutions
- Scope too large: ambition exceeds what's feasible in 12-18 months with OSIP budget
- Scope too small: the proposed work is trivial or could be a student project
- Missing TRL assessment: no clear indication of current maturity and target
- Duplicates existing work: similar activity already funded by ESA or national agencies
- No ESA relevance: technology is interesting but no clear connection to ESA missions
- Budget unrealistic: either too low (not credible) or too high (exceeds OSIP limits)
- Marketing language: proposal reads like a sales pitch, not a technical document
- Missing validation plan: no description of how results will be verified
- Weak team fit: no evidence the proposer can deliver (implicit in writing quality)
- Wrong TRL range: OSIP targets TRL 1-4 typically; proposals at TRL 6+ don't fit
