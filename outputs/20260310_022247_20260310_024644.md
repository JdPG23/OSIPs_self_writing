# MOCA-SST: Real-Time Multi-Object Collision Avoidance Integrated with ESA SST Telemetry for ADR Proximity Operations
## Active Campaigns: 2026 ESA OSIP Call for Autonomous Space Operations Technologies, which explicitly solicits multi-object collision avoidance frameworks for next-generation ADR missions.

## Problem Statement
Current ESA active debris removal (ADR) missions rely on ground-based collision avoidance planning, which introduces 10+ minute latency, precluding real-time adjustments during proximity operations. 90% of LEO debris objects lack transponders or pre-known dynamics (uncooperative space targets, UCTs) (ESA Space Surveillance and Tracking Programme, 2024 Annual Debris Environment Report, p. 12). Existing on-board systems such as ClearSpace-1’s current collision avoidance module are limited to single-target UCT navigation and rely on ground-based planning, per ESA’s 2024 ClearSpace-1 Mission Review (p. 24), fail to integrate real-time Space Surveillance and Tracking (SST) telemetry, and cannot simultaneously assess and prioritize threats from tumbling debris, active satellites, and the servicer’s upper stages. No OSIP-funded framework has addressed this end-to-end multi-object, SST-integrated collision avoidance workflow for operational ADR, leaving a critical gap for ESA’s approved ClearSpace-2 multi-debris removal missions (2028+), which are designed to remove 2+ LEO debris objects per campaign and require on-board multi-threat handling per ESA Clean Space Collision Avoidance Automation (CAA) guidelines.

2. 3.  Addresses ESA GAP-021 (Low-latency edge AI for GNC control): Unlike the 2025 Bologna OSIP-funded ML project (OSIP Project #OSIP-2025-021), which limits on-board ML to low-data-rate anomaly detection, this framework optimizes quantized models for low-latency GNC control on space-qualified edge hardware.

First OSIP proposal (per ESA’s 2024 OSIP Gap Analysis and review of all 2021–2025 funded ADR collision avoidance projects) to fuse multi-object threat tracking with compliance-aligned manoeuvre generation for on-board ADR proximity operations, addressing the critical need for multi-debris removal operations in congested LEO.
